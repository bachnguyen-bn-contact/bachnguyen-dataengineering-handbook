# Community resources

Beyond Microsoft's own material, a few community members publish genuinely useful, well-maintained DP-700 guides. These are good for a second perspective and for mapping the skills list to specific docs.

## Worth your time

- **Kevin Chant — DP-700 checklist.** A regularly updated checklist that maps the exam objectives to resources and links. A solid companion to the official study guide. ([kevinrchant.com](https://www.kevinrchant.com/))
- **Andy Cutler — Skills Measured resources.** Walks the official skills-measured list and pairs each item with the relevant Microsoft docs and Learn modules. ([serverlesssql.com](https://www.serverlesssql.com/))
- **Adam Marczak — free video course.** A free, well-regarded YouTube walkthrough of the Fabric syllabus (architecture, lakehouse, pipelines, notebooks, real-time). Good if you prefer watching to reading.
- **Exam-taker write-ups.** Several community members (e.g. Sam Debruyn) have published "here's what the exam was like" reflections — useful for calibrating expectations.

> Links to community sites are starting points; people move and rename pages. If one's dead, search the author's name plus "DP-700" and you'll find their latest.

## A word on "dumps"

You'll run into sites selling **exam dumps** — supposedly real exam questions. Skip them. They violate Microsoft's exam policy (and can get your certification revoked), they're frequently wrong or out of date, and they teach you to recognise questions rather than do the job. **Practice questions with explanations** are fine and useful; *verbatim leaked questions* are not. Stick to the official practice assessment and reputable question banks that explain their answers.

## The honest summary

The official study guide plus hands-on time in a Fabric trial will get most people to a pass. Community resources are there to fill gaps and give you confidence — not to replace doing the work.
