# Official resources

Anchor your prep on Microsoft's own material first. Everything below is free and kept current with the platform.

## Start here

- **[Exam DP-700 study guide](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/credentials/certifications/resources/study-guides/dp-700)** — the authoritative skills-measured outline. Print it, and check off each bullet as you cover it. Note the *"skills measured as of"* date at the top.
- **[Fabric Data Engineer Associate certification page](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/credentials/certifications/fabric-data-engineer-associate/)** — overview, scheduling, and the official **free practice assessment**.

## Learn the platform

- **[Microsoft Fabric documentation](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/fabric/)** — the full docs, available to you in a split-screen *during* the exam, so get comfortable navigating them now.
- **[Get started with Microsoft Fabric (learning path)](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/training/paths/get-started-fabric/)** — guided modules covering lakehouse, warehouse, Real-Time Intelligence and more.

## Use the practice assessment well

Microsoft's free practice assessment mirrors the real exam's style and difficulty (not the actual questions). The trick is to take it **twice**:

1. **Early**, before you've studied much — to find your weak domains.
2. **At the end** — to confirm you're ready across all three.

It's an underused resource. Don't skip it.

## Get your hands dirty

Reading isn't enough for this exam. Spin up a Fabric trial and build:

- your **first lakehouse**, ingesting and transforming sample data;
- a **pipeline** and a **Dataflow Gen2** doing the same load, so you understand the trade-offs;
- a tiny **Eventstream + KQL** query, if real-time is new to you.
